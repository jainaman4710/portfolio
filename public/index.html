Method

My Methodology

When a new problem lands, I start by understanding it, then check whether I already have the baseline knowledge to move on: knowledge of the deliverable itself, and knowledge of the industry it sits in. If I don't, I build that up first. Either way, I move into a deep dive on the context, the data, or the core of the problem and everything around it, then search out the directions I could take and explore them one by one. I pick the strongest direction and go deep there, that becomes the core of the work. From there it's refinement, filling gaps, sometimes finding a better solution along the way, before the output goes out the door with a clear sense of what to expect from it. Feedback comes too, though how much depends on the project.

  • Applied SMOTE oversampling and feature engineering across 23 raw features to surface behavioural transaction signals, after establishing that high-amount outliers are genuine fraud indicators rather than noise.

Passive TMD: Trailer Sway Mitigation

Trailer sway causes roughly 5-7% of all road accidents and 18,000+ annual incidents in India, with current solutions (electronic stability systems, weight-distribution hitches, speed limits) all requiring either power, driver skill, or restrictive trade-offs that fail at the moment sway actually begins.

  • Identified that trailer oscillation amplitude grows by roughly 40% per second once past a critical threshold, leaving a narrow multi-second window before fishtailing escalates to jackknifing or rollover at highway speed.
  • Adapted tuned mass damper principles from tall-building wind and seismic stabilisation into a zero-power, fully mechanical multi-point damping system that responds at the instant of sway onset, with no electrical failure mode.
  • Built for IIT KGP General Championship Hardware Modelling 2025 under Meghnad Saha Hall, with a comparative analysis showing the passive system eliminates the activation delay inherent to electronic stability systems.
