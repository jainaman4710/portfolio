Problem Statement

The Strategic Brief: Scaling Desire

Designing a Profitable and Defensible D2C Fragrance Brand in India — IIT Kharagpur General Championship Case Study 2026

Case Background

India’s beauty and personal care market is evolving rapidly, and fragrance is emerging as one of its most aspiration-driven categories. For a growing base of young, digital-first consumers, perfume is not merely a product but an extension of identity, self-expression, and lifestyle.

This shift has enabled the rise of digital-first fragrance brands operating through D2C channels, leveraging performance marketing, influencer-led storytelling, and promotional mechanics to drive rapid growth. However, fragrance remains structurally complex: sensory, trial-driven, and emotionally anchored. Trial does not automatically translate into loyalty. Promotional intensity can drive short-term sales while weakening long-term pricing power.

The Central Strategic Question

“Can Amorist scale into a profitable, differentiated, and high-retention fragrance brand without becoming structurally dependent on discounting and short-term acquisition tactics?”

This case is not about maximizing short-term sales. It is about designing a fragrance brand that customers return to repeatedly — one that balances aspiration, pricing power, and retention. Amorist’s challenge is to transition from high-conversion momentum to durable brand equity and profitable scale.
Current Company Context: Amorist Perfumes
Offers full-size perfumes with visible list prices alongside promotional selling prices
Sells 15ml formats positioned for discovery and trial at ₹249 (margin-negative)
Communicates premium cues such as longevity claims and certification assurances
Uses promotional levers: bundle discounts, cart-based offers, and multi-buy incentives
Operates primarily through a D2C storefront with perpetual 50% markdowns on 100ml flagship
Early traction and durable brand equity are not synonymous. The brand now stands at a strategic inflection point.
Structural Risks of the Current Model
Discount Dependency: Continuous markdowns train consumers to perceive discounted price as the true value
Weak Price Integrity: MRP becomes a vanity metric; brand loses long-term pricing power permanently
Low Repeat Purchase Rates: Deal-seekers churn the moment any competitor offers a steeper discount
Margin Compression: High CAC + reduced RSP + free shipping leaves near-zero contribution margin
Shallow Brand Loyalty: No emotional switching costs; customers are acquired, not retained

The Evaporation Paradox: Indian summer heat accelerates molecular motion → flash evaporation of top notes → light scents disappear in under 1 hour. Import formulas fail. Solution: 18–20% aromatic concentrate as EDP + base note fixatives with high boiling points that bond to skin (Ambroxan, Dry Vetiver, White Musk).

Naming Strategy — Cross-Sensory Mapping (Synesthesia Principle): Names like Slate, Velvet, and Suede trigger tactile sensations digitally, bypassing the inability to smell through a screen. Gamified Loyalty — “The Amorist Inner Sanctum”
📚 Behavioural Psychology: Variable Reward Theory
67% of Gen Z feel lonely and crave connection (McKinsey, 2023). A purpose-driven community loyalty program is the competitive moat. As members ascend tiers, rewards shift from monetary discounts to exclusive non-monetary soft benefits, fostering emotional connection without margin sacrifice. This is grounded in Variable Reward Theory (Skinner, 1938): unpredictable, non-monetary rewards drive stronger habit formation than predictable discounts.

Risk & Mitigation

Navigating the Pivot

Safeguarding brand equity and retention during the transition to a value-led model through calculated trade-offs

⚠ Risk Identified
✓ Mitigation Strategy
Risk 01
High Entry Price Barrier
Transitioning from an accessible ₹249 standalone 15ml to an ₹899 Discovery Set creates a steeper financial barrier for new customer acquisition. Cold traffic may not convert at the higher entry point.
Acquisition Risk
→
Strategy 01
AI Chatbot + Tier 1 GWP Code
Drive traffic to an AI chatbot that recommends fragrance profiles based on user lifestyle. Upon completion, users receive a unique code for a Tier 1 GWP (Aromatic Refresh Wipes) to redeem with their first purchase. Reduces friction while preserving value framing.

✓ Mitigation Strategy
Risk 02
Sales Decline from Discount Removal
Removing perpetual 50% markdowns may alienate price-sensitive cohorts who formed the primary customer base. Short-term revenue impact is expected and will be significant, creating pressure to revert to the old model.
Revenue Risk
→
Strategy 02
Calculated Trade-Off: This is the Point
The short-term sales decline is intentional. Eliminating the Discount Death Spiral means deliberately shedding low-retention, price-sensitive buyers in favour of a value-led cohort. Existing customers receive complimentary sidecar vials with every 100ml Hero purchase to maintain Affective Loyalty (Oliver Stage 2) during the transition.
Deliberate Trade-Off
⚠ Risk Identified
✓ Mitigation Strategy
Risk 03
Format Cannibalization
Consumers may perpetually repurchase the ₹849 50ml bridge format instead of upgrading to the ₹1,499 100ml Hero tier, permanently anchoring in the lower-margin format and suppressing the portfolio’s AOV ceiling.
Portfolio Risk
→
Strategy 03
Scent Collector Bonus via Inner Sanctum
The Inner Sanctum loyalty program rewards portfolio depth. A “Scent Collector” mechanic awards 500 bonus points for every new fragrance purchased in the 100ml Hero format, creating strong gamified incentives to upgrade. This applies Variable Reward Theory (Skinner) to push consumers up the value ladder without discounting.
