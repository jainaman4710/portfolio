01 · Context

The Business Problem

ElectroMart's CFO raised concerns: over the previous 12 months, the company spent ₹8.46B on marketing while generating only ₹4.04B in revenue, meaning spend outpaced revenue by more than 2×. Only 1 of 12 months saw revenue exceed that month's marketing spend. The question was whether to cut the budget, or prove that smarter allocation of the same budget could turn this around.

Company Profile

ElectroMart is an Ontario, Canada-based e-commerce firm specialising in consumer electronics, cameras, gaming hardware, speakers, and home audio. They run large-scale promotions (Big Billion Day analogues) and invest heavily in multi-channel advertising: TV, digital, SEM, sponsorship, affiliates, and more. Canada's e-commerce market is on track to exceed $130B by 2027, making smart allocation critical.

Ontario, Canada Electronics E-Commerce B2C Jul 2023–Jun 2024

Three Core Objectives

01

Performance Driver Analysis

Which KPIs drive top-line GMV? Used distance correlation (dCor) to capture non-linear dependencies, going beyond Pearson to detect saturation, threshold, and compounding effects.

02

Marketing ROI Impact

Quantify the revenue elasticity of each of 9 advertising channels via a log-linear Ridge Regression model. Log transform captures diminishing returns; Ridge handles multicollinearity between highly correlated channels.

03

Budget Optimisation

Apply constrained gradient-ascent optimisation, 100 random restarts, to maximise projected GMV subject to a fixed ₹8.46B total budget with per-channel min/max constraints.

Cleaning & Imputation Methods

  • Replaced "\N" and empty strings in deliverybdays/deliverycdays with 0 per case specification
  • Missing GMV imputed via median GMV-per-unit grouped by FSN ID, preserves product-level pricing signal
  • Weather nulls: 3-prior + 3-next rolling average, ensures temporal consistency (MICE also evaluated but local rolling outperformed it on stationarity tests)
  • IQR filtering on GMV and delivery time, removed distorting extreme outliers without losing distribution shape
  • Postal code validation, eliminated geographic inconsistencies in location-based features
  • All timestamps standardised to %Y-%m-%d uniform format

Feature Engineering, 34 Final Columns

  • discount_pct = 100 × (MRP − list_price) / MRP, actual markdown per order
  • total_delivery_days = deliverybdays + deliverycdays, end-to-end fulfillment time
  • delivery_delay_vs_sla, difference from contracted SLA target
  • is_weekend, is_holiday, sales_mark, is_special_date, binary temporal flags
  • gmv_category = "Luxury" if GMV > 80th percentile, else "Normal"
  • Monthly order frequency per customer; first-order vs. repeat-order flags from customer purchase history
  • SKU merged with product hierarchy enabling Category, Sub_Category, Vertical groupings
  • elapsed_time_minutes, minutes since first order in dataset; captures overall trend

🌡️ Weather Impact Discovery

A threshold effect was identified: delivery delays spike sharply above 3.5°C, not during extreme cold. This counterintuitive finding reflects order volume surges in warm months outpacing logistics capacity, rather than weather directly disrupting shipping.

Implication: Pre-scale logistics before warm season. The 3.5°C threshold provides a data-driven trigger for activating contingency logistics plans.

Sales Timing Patterns (2-Hour Heatmap)

  • Peak sales: 10am–2pm consistent across all months
  • Secondary peak: 8pm–10pm, consistent with mobile browsing after work hours
  • Overnight (12am–6am): lowest volume but non-zero, making it a candidate for automated re-marketing
  • Sales event days show sharp intraday concentration vs. distributed daily pattern on normal days

Why Distance Correlation (dCor)?

  • Pearson correlation only captures linear relationships, which is insufficient for marketing data exhibiting saturation curves, thresholds, and interaction effects
  • dCor detects any statistical dependency including non-linear and non-monotonic ones; zero dCor implies true independence
  • Key finding: NPS emerged as a stronger revenue driver than Pearson suggested, because satisfaction has compounding, lagged future revenue effects that linear measures miss
  • VIF analysis confirmed no problematic multicollinearity among KPIs (no VIF > 5 found)

05 · Modelling

Ridge Regression + Hypothesis Testing

A log-linear model was built to capture diminishing marginal returns, reflecting the economic reality that doubling ad spend does not double revenue. Ridge regularisation handles multicollinearity between highly correlated channels (SEM and Digital: r=0.97; Online and Affiliates: r=0.99).

Per-Channel Revenue Model (Log-Linear)
R_i = a_i + b_i · log(S_i)
Aggregate Revenue (9 channels)
R_total = Σ(i=1→9) [a_i + b_i · log(S_i)]
Where
R_i = revenue from channel i | S_i = spend on channel i
a_i, b_i = Ridge regression coefficients (α = 0.14)
Hypothesis Test (t-statistic)
t = β / SE(β)  →  Hessian H = ∂²J(w)/∂w²
Cov(w) = H⁻¹  →  σ² = diag(H⁻¹)
F-Statistic
F = Explained Variance / Unexplained Variance
F_computed = 6.70 | F_critical (5%) = 19.38

Why Log Transformation?

  • Marketing exhibits diminishing returns: early ad spend is highly effective, but each incremental dollar yields less as the audience saturates, a core microeconomic principle
  • Log-transform linearises this relationship for OLS/Ridge without losing the underlying economic structure
  • Eliminates heteroscedasticity in residuals, confirmed by visual inspection of residual plots
  • All significant pairs of log-transformed columns have p < 1e-12, confirming strong log-linear relationships

Why Ridge Regression?

  • SEM and Digital correlation: r = 0.97; Online and Affiliates: r = 0.99. This level of multicollinearity makes OLS coefficients unstable and unreliable
  • Ridge adds L2 penalty (λ‖β‖²) shrinking correlated coefficients toward each other → stable, interpretable estimates across all 9 channels simultaneously
  • α = 0.14 selected via GridSearchCV over [0.001, 0.01, 0.1, 1, 10, 100]; k-fold CV confirmed generalisability
  • Hessian-based uncertainty quantification validates coefficient reliability across channels

Optimisation Design Choices

  • Gradient ascent chosen over scipy minimize for speed and transparency, with rapid convergence well-suited to marketing planning timelines
  • 100 random restarts used to avoid local optima; best solution selected by maximum objective value
  • Normalisation constraint ensures Σwᵢ = 1 at every step, so the budget is never exceeded
  • Channel min/max bounds reflect real-world operational constraints: no active channel is fully zeroed, and no single channel absorbs the entire budget
  • Sensitivity analysis confirmed model robustness across ±20% budget variation scenarios
  • Digital receives near-zero allocation, consistent with its near-zero ridge coefficient (0.07M) and negative Pearson correlation with revenue (-0.55)

07 · Operations

Inventory Optimisation

Warehouse unit allocation was optimised across 14 product sub-categories using a weighted objective combining procurement SLA efficiency and actual sales contribution percentage. Gradient descent converged in just 3 iterations.

Inventory Objective Function
Maximise: Σ(i=1→14) [SLA_i × Sale%_i × Units_i]
Where:
SLA_i = weighted avg procurement SLA for sub-cat i
Sale%_i = sub-category % contribution to total GMV
Units_i = warehouse units allocated
Gradient Descent Update
y ← y + α · ∇f(y), α = 0.001
Converged in 3 iterations | Optimal value: ₹5.70B

Design Rationale

  • SLA weight captures procurement difficulty: faster-to-procure items should be stocked more aggressively to reduce stockout risk
  • Sales % ensures allocation reflects the actual demand signal from real order data
  • Joint multiplication penalises high-SLA, low-sales categories from consuming disproportionate warehouse space
  • Gradient descent preferred over CVXPY LP, faster for this non-linear formulation with 14 variables and achieving the theoretical optimum with standard convergence
  • GameMembershipCards receive ~0% allocation: digital fulfilment products require no physical warehouse space

08 · Recommendations

Strategic Recommendations

Data-driven recommendations across marketing strategy, product targeting, channel selection, and operations, directly derived from model outputs and EDA findings.

🎯 Products to Target in Campaigns

Primary targets: DSLR cameras (35.4% GMV), Speakers (9.9%), Point & Shoot cameras (7.3%). Highest absolute revenue contribution, campaigns here generate proportionate returns.

Growth targets: Gaming Consoles and TVVideoSmall, data shows strong sub-category growth trajectory; ideal for next-quarter campaign expansion before competition intensifies.

De-prioritise: GameMembershipCards (digital) and AmplifierReceiver. Both show minimal sales contribution relative to campaign cost, and their low SLA-weighted inventory scores reinforce this.

📡 Channel Strategy by Product Category

SEM + Content Marketing for Camera/DSLR: High-intent search traffic converts well for premium, considered-purchase electronics. How-to content and review-driven pieces support informed buyer journeys.

Sponsorship + Online Mktg → Speakers & Gaming: Brand awareness and influencer/affiliate campaigns align with the younger demographic and discovery-driven purchasing behaviour of gaming and audio products.

TV + Radio for seasonal peaks only (Nov–Dec): High reach but lowest elasticity year-round. Best reserved for broad awareness during major promotional events such as Black Friday and Boxing Day, when audience saturation temporarily resets.

Budget Reallocation Actions

  • Increase Content Marketing by +22%, highest Ridge coefficient at 101.6M
  • Increase SEM allocation to 17.8%, second-highest elasticity at 96.0M
  • Reduce Digital to near-zero; Ridge coefficient ≈ 0.07M and Pearson correlation of -0.55 both indicate negligible revenue impact
  • Reduce Radio by 50%; t-value of 4,577 vs. 10,080 for SEM reflects substantially lower confidence in its revenue returns
  • Stabilise Sponsorship at 16%; strong Pearson correlation (0.92) with GMV confirms its continued relevance
  • Increase Affiliates to 12.9%; correlation of 0.96 with revenue suggests significant untapped leverage

Operational Improvements

  • Pre-scale logistics capacity before April–June (warm season, where the 3.5°C delay trigger activates)
  • Negotiate tighter supplier procurement SLA contracts to reduce σ = 53.46 variance; high unpredictability creates stockout and overstock cycles
  • Schedule campaign bursts on weekends, +19% GMV lift is already present organically, amplify with targeted push
  • Reallocate warehouse: 46% Games, 25% Camera, 8% Speaker per optimised inventory model
  • Improve prepaid checkout UX; a COD:Prepaid GMV ratio of 1.41× suggests prepaid is under-leveraged despite its lower operational risk

Future Research & Model Extensions

  • Integrate macroeconomic indicators (CAD inflation, GDP, consumer confidence) to refine spending elasticity estimates, especially for the luxury/DSLR segment
  • Apply Reinforcement Learning for dynamic real-time budget allocation, where each period updates the model with new revenue observations
  • Multi-touch attribution modelling to quantify cross-channel interaction effects (TV awareness → SEM conversion funnel)
  • Build a customer churn prediction model using behavioural sequences; at 95.3%, churn represents a major growth lever if reduced even modestly
  • Develop live Tableau dashboard with real-time data feed for month-over-month optimisation loop
