Critical points in supply chain steps

Core manufacturing
High incidence of labour rights violations: excessive overtime, workplace abuse, high turnover
Significant energy and water consumption, contributing heavily to industry emissions
Generation of hazardous waste (chemicals, heavy metals) requiring strict disposal protocols
Assembly and packaging
Overuse of plastics and non-recyclable materials leads to substantial packaging waste
Persistent labour issues: lack of social benefits and wage theft among temporary workers
Balancing protective packaging needs against sustainability and recyclability standards
Testing
High risk of counterfeit components entering the supply chain, threatening product quality
Poor-quality components increase failure rates and environmental impact from frequent replacements
Limited integration of ethical sourcing verification in standard testing protocols
Component sourcing
Lack of visibility over tier 2 and tier 3 suppliers increases exposure to unethical sourcing
Supplier data is often fragmented, outdated, and difficult to verify, hindering traceability
Cost and resource constraints make robust tracking systems difficult for SMEs
Aftermarket services
End-of-life processes generate significant e-waste often containing toxic substances
Limited infrastructure and inconsistent quality for recycling and remanufacturing
Short product lifecycles and lack of spare parts contribute to increased waste
Enterprise logistics
Inefficiencies in inventory, warehousing, and transport lead to excess resource use
Poor internal coordination results in resource wastage and higher environmental impact
Organisational resistance and lack of integration slow adoption of sustainable practices

Solutions framework

Equity investments in certified green companies
100% LTCG exemption on shares held 24+ months in green-certified companies. Aims to channel private capital toward low-carbon startups in India.
Qualifying companies listed on BSE/NSE or via SEBI-compliant private placements
Max investment of Rs 25 lakh per investor per financial year
Up to Rs 1 lakh profits per year tax-exempt
Green Registry certification number required for automatic exemption
Mechanism
Green Registry Portal (SEBI/MoEFCC) verifies status every 3 years via audited environmental data and on-site audits.
Certified green bonds
Interest income on certified green bonds exempt up to Rs 2 lakh/year. Full LTCG exemption on bonds held 36+ months. Issued by IREDA, REC, PSUs or private issuers.
Maximum of Rs 50 lakh per individual investor annually
Issuers apply 0% TDS with valid KYC and declaration
Special "Green Code" or ISIN tagging for automatic exemptions
Proceeds ring-fenced for solar parks, EVs, afforestation
Target sectors
Solar parks, electric mobility, afforestation, and circular economy projects requiring large-scale patient capital.
