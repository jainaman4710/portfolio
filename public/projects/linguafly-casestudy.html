01 Vision & Mission

The North Star

Every product starts with a clear articulation of what it is trying to achieve and who it serves. The vision defines the ultimate destination; the mission defines the path.

Vision Statement

To become the go-to language companion for students navigating new countries · empowering every learner to communicate confidently in any language, anywhere in the world.

✓ Target audience defined ✓ Problem addressed ✓ Ultimate goal stated ✓ Concise, single sentence

Mission Statement

LinguaFly delivers fast, structured, and destination-aware language learning · helping students going abroad survive and thrive in a foreign-language environment, while helping domestic learners build systematic communication skills for academic and professional growth.

✓ How vision is achieved ✓ Both segments covered ✓ Language-agnostic framing
🎯
Core Product Insight

1.32 million Indian students study abroad annually. Over 25% land in countries where neither English nor their native language is the daily medium. Most apps treat a student leaving in 8 weeks identically to a hobbyist with 2 years. LinguaFly's differentiation is urgency-awareness and destination-specificity · a gap no competitor currently fills.

04 User Research

What Users Actually Said

Interviews were conducted with users matching both personas. Patterns across pain points, learning preferences, and app usage habits were documented and synthesized into product decisions.

Pain Points Identified
01

No milestone transparency

Users didn't know how long it would take to reach functional conversation vs. fluency. They need upfront time-anchored estimates to plan commitment.

02

Inaccurate starting point

Customisation at sign-up didn't genuinely change difficulty. Users with prior knowledge still received beginner content · frustrating and demotivating.

03

Script blindness

Users learning Arabic or Japanese could speak phonetically but couldn't read the script · a confidence gap that caused motivation loss mid-course.

04

Inflexible daily caps

On free days, users wanted to learn more but apps served the same repeated vocabulary in loops instead of new content beyond the daily quota.

05

Notification timing mismatch

Student schedules are irregular. Fixed-time reminders were dismissed repeatedly, breaking streaks unfairly. Users wanted pattern-aware nudges.

Preferences and Habits
Pace Preference

Several users found standard lesson pace too slow. They want an optional fast-track mode that covers more material per session without sacrificing quality.

Flexibility vs Structure

Users want a soft daily quota with an optional extended mode · but also a session timer so they don't accidentally spend more time than intended.

Smart Notifications

Students requested activity-pattern-based reminders. The app should learn when they are typically free, not rely on a manually set time that becomes stale.

Script Learning

Users want a dedicated optional track for scripts running parallel to vocabulary · especially for Arabic (RTL), Japanese hiragana/katakana, and German capitalization.

05 Feature Brainstorming and Prioritization

What Gets Built

Features were ideated from user research, competitor gap analysis, and original thinking. Prioritized using Kano model, MoSCoW framework, and RICE scoring for the must-have set.

Original Features · Beyond Competitor Consolidation
🤖

AI Conversation Partner

A built-in AI chatbot for practicing full conversations in the target language. Solves the "I have no one to practice with" problem · especially for shy learners. The AI responds, corrects, and adapts in real time.

🏆

Social Leaderboards with Friends

Users add friends and compete on weekly XP leaderboards. Unlike anonymous leagues, this is friend-specific · creating personal accountability and genuine peer motivation rather than competition with strangers.

📅

Departure Mode

User enters a departure date. The app auto-calculates a milestone plan · what they'll be able to say by week 2, week 4, and week 8. Directly addresses the milestone transparency pain point identified in research.

🎯

Situational Lesson Packs

Pre-built bundles for real scenarios: "At the German university registration desk", "At an Arabic supermarket", "Taking a bus in Riyadh". High utility for destination-specific learners who need survival phrases over CEFR levels.

📊

Milestone Progress Dashboard

Visual tracker showing estimated time to each level, words learned vs. target, and skill breakdown by listening, speaking, reading, and vocabulary. Replaces vague "34% done" with time-anchored milestones.

🔤

Script Fast-Track Mode

A parallel track for learning Arabic (RTL script) or Japanese (hiragana/katakana) · separate from vocabulary lessons. Performance-based review surfaces forgotten characters automatically, solving script blindness.

Risk Register
🔴 High · R1

Base API Delay

If the Base API is not completed in the first 3 weeks of July, all downstream engineering tasks cascade. A 1-week slip here causes a 2-week slip downstream · threatening the Beta date.

Begin API development on Day 1 (Jul 1). Assign senior engineer. Flag as a weekly checkpoint · not just a sprint item.
🔴 High · R2

Beta Feedback Volume Too Low

If the limited Beta doesn't attract enough testers, the post-Beta bug-fix sprint will lack signal · weakening final MVP quality before public launch.

Pre-register 50+ Beta users by Aug 1 via waitlist. Target university networks (IIT, MNIT communities) that match the core personas directly.
🟡 Medium · R3

Language Content Not Ready

Language experts creating English and Arabic lessons work in parallel to engineering. If content isn't handed off by Jul 25, lesson modules cannot be integrated before the Beta.

Set a hard content handoff deadline of Jul 25. Start with a minimum of 30 lessons · enough to demonstrate a viable Beta experience.
🟡 Medium · R4

Scope Creep Post-Beta

Stakeholders may request additional features between Beta and MVP launch. Adding unplanned work in this window risks pushing the launch date.

Enforce a feature freeze from Aug 15. Any new requests go to the Q4 backlog. Communicate this boundary clearly and upfront before Beta launches.
🟡 Medium · R5

Notification System Accuracy

Activity-pattern notifications require user behavior data. At MVP stage, users may not have built habits yet · making smart notifications appear random or poorly timed.

Ship basic time-based notifications in MVP. Roll out activity-pattern AI in v1.1 once behavioral data exists (4 to 6 weeks post-launch).
🟢 Low · R6

App Store Approval Delay

Google Play and Apple App Store reviews can take 3 to 7 days. A submission close to the target date may not clear in time for the public launch announcement.

Submit to both stores by Sep 23. Target Sep 30 as the public launch announcement date · build in a 7-day approval buffer.
08 Refine, Communicate and Living Document

After the Roadmap

The PM identifies risks and dependencies before the stakeholder meeting · not during it. The feedback session validates the PM's pre-existing analysis. A living document protocol ensures the roadmap evolves responsibly and transparently.

PM Approach · Validation, Not Discovery

Coming to a stakeholder review without pre-identified risks and dependencies signals poor preparation. The correct approach: the PM arrives with dependencies mapped and risks rated (done in Section 07 above). The stakeholder meeting then validates, challenges, and refines the PM's analysis. Questions to stakeholders are oriented around confirmation · not open-ended discovery that puts the PM on the back foot.

PM Pre-Meeting Preparation

  • All 4 work stream dependencies mapped and documented (Section 07)
  • 6 risks identified, rated by severity, and mitigations proposed
  • Timeline reviewed for internal consistency · no task starts before its dependency completes
  • Feature list cross-checked: only MVP-prioritized features appear in current scope
  • PM's own confidence estimate per feature cluster scored 1 to 5 before asking stakeholders
  • Change log template prepared for living document protocol

Stakeholder Review Questions

  • I have mapped these 6 dependencies · are there any I've missed from your team's side?
  • I've rated these 6 risks · do any look higher or lower severity to you based on your experience?
  • On a scale of 1 to 5, how confident are you in Beta readiness by the target date?
  • On a scale of 1 to 5, is the time allotted per feature appropriate? (3 = just right, 5 = too much)
  • Are there any human resource constraints for Q3 I should factor in?
  • Any concerns about the MVP launch date from your team's side?

Living Document Protocol
01

Triggers for Revision

The roadmap is updated · not replaced · when any of the following occur:

  • Beta user feedback reveals a Must-Have was wrong or missing
  • A technical blocker pushes a feature to Q4
  • A competitor releases a feature that changes market priority
  • New user research updates persona understanding
  • Stakeholder priority shift changes business goals
  • Content delivery misses the Jul 25 hard deadline
02

Version Control Process

Every roadmap change is documented with: version number, date changed, what changed, why it changed, and who approved it. This prevents silent scope changes that cause team confusion.

Changes are communicated to all stakeholders within 24 hours of approval. A changelog is pinned at the top of the shared roadmap document · always visible to the whole team.

Minor changes (sub-feature re-sequencing within the same sprint) can be made by the PM unilaterally with documentation. Major changes require stakeholder sign-off.

03

Escalation Protocol

If a change affects a Must-Have feature, the Beta date, or the MVP launch date · it requires sign-off from all key stakeholders, not just the PM.

If the MVP date is at genuine risk, a full re-planning session is called within 48 hours of the blocker being identified. The PM comes to that session with 2 to 3 concrete options, not an open question.

All decisions and rationale are documented in the changelog for future retrospective reference.
