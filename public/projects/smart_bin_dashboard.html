Problem Statement

Context
⚠
The Core Problem
Trash bins overflow because they are not emptied frequently enough, cannot hold sufficient waste, and waste is not separated properly. This makes cities and villages dirty and spreads diseases across communities.
🦟
Health Risks
Overflowing trash attracts insects and mosquitoes, spreading typhoid and malaria. Dust and gases from decomposing waste cause respiratory illness. Stray animals are injured or killed from ingesting plastic.
💧
Hygiene & Environment
During monsoon, overflow washes into rivers and lakes, contaminating water used for drinking and agriculture. This creates cascading public health risks across both urban and rural populations.
₹
Economic Burden
Cleaning up overflowing waste consumes a disproportionate share of municipal budgets. Inefficient collection routing and bin overflow together raise operational costs far beyond what is necessary.

Existing Solutions & Limitations

Analysis
Swachh Bharat Mission / Dual-bin system Existing
✓ Green bin (wet waste) + Blue bin (dry waste) segregation rollout across municipalities
✓ Encourages citizen participation and community ownership of cleanliness
✗ Entirely dependent on citizen compliance and attitude, not enforceable by design
✗ No mechanism to increase bin capacity or reduce overflow frequency
SBM / Bin every 500m initiative Existing
✓ Increases proximity of bins to citizens, reducing littering from inconvenience
✓ Raises visibility of waste infrastructure in public spaces
✗ Multiplying bins without improving capacity management scales the problem
✗ No efficient waste compaction or smart collection mechanism in place
Gap identified

Neither existing solution addresses the root engineering problem: bins fill up faster than they are collected. Our product targets this gap directly. By compressing waste at the point of disposal, we effectively double bin capacity and extend the viable collection interval, independent of citizen behaviour.

Product Overview

Design
Core feature
Compaction System
Worm gear + lead screw mechanism compresses waste, minimising air voids and maximising usable bin volume. Operated by cleaning staff via a hand wheel.
Security feature
Human-only Lid
Sophisticated lid mechanism restricts access to humans only, preventing stray animal interference and reducing litter spread around the bin.
Maintenance feature
Side-exit Clearance
Internal container slides out sideways (not lifted from the top), allowing easy emptying into waste trucks and simple removal for cleaning.
Target deployment

The product is designed for commercial zones in Swachh Bharat Mission cities, where household waste systems are established but commercial waste relies only on static open bins. These high-footfall areas generate the greatest overflow frequency and benefit most from compaction.

Mechanisms & Working Principle

Engineering
Compression mechanism
01
Waste deposit
User lifts lid and deposits waste into the bin. Bin fills progressively over time via normal use.
02
Staff actuates wheel
Cleaning staff rotates the hand wheel, transmitting rotational force into the worm gear assembly.
03
Force amplification
The worm gear (steel worm + brass wheel) amplifies the input force by a factor of 50, changing the plane of rotation by 90°.
04
Lead screw drives piston
Amplified torque drives the lead screw, pushing the piston downward. Air voids collapse; waste density rises from 314 to ~650 kg/m³.
Garbage clearing mechanism
01
Side-opening design
Unlike conventional top-lift bins, the outer casing opens sideways when the waste truck arrives for collection.
02
Interior container slides out
The interior container is mounted on caster wheels and slides out laterally, eliminating the need to lift heavy compacted waste.
03
Emptied into truck
Container contents are discharged into the waste dumping truck. The container is reinserted and the outer cover re-locked.
Key component roles
Lead screwHigh-force linear actuation, large mechanical advantage
Worm gear90° force redirection + 50× amplification
Caster wheelsForged steel, container mobility

Safety constraint – Li-ion batteries

E-waste in bins may contain Li-ion cells that rupture above 30–35 kPa. The 30 kN maximum force, distributed across the bin's compaction area, remains within safe limits. No thermal runaway risk is introduced by normal operation.

Cost & Weight Analysis

Analysis
Weight methodology

Weight is calculated from first principles using material density and actual surface/volume estimates for each component. The HDPE container uses 3 mm wall thickness across its surface area (19.5 m²) at 950 kg/m³. The outer cover is mild steel sheet at 1 mm gauge over a partial enclosure area (~12 m²) at 7,850 kg/m³. The main mechanism accounts for a 4 mm mild-steel piston plate (1.5 m × 2.5 m), scissor-lift frame members, worm gearbox, lead screw assembly, support columns, and hand wheel shaft. This approach yields a total unit weight of ~325 kg, which is consistent with comparable industrial compactor bins of this volume (250–500 kg range per industry reference).

Future Prospects

Roadmap
01
IoT & solar automation
Solar-powered sensors monitor fill levels and transmit real-time alerts to the municipal authority. Enables dynamic route optimisation for collection vehicles, reducing fuel consumption and ensuring timely emptying before overflow occurs. IoT integration also automates bin compaction at scheduled intervals.
Phase 2 · IoT / Solar
02
Biogas generation chamber
A dedicated compartment separates organic waste and initiates anaerobic decomposition to produce biogas. The generated biogas is stored and made available to local municipal bodies as a supplementary, sustainable energy source, converting a waste problem into an energy asset.
Phase 3 · Renewable energy
03
Automated disinfectant chamber
An integrated chamber releases disinfectant at programmed intervals, maintaining hygiene inside the bin without requiring manual cleaning. This reduces pathogen build-up, odour, and insect attraction, particularly important in high-footfall commercial deployment locations.
Phase 3 · Hygiene tech
