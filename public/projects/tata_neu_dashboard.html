03
UI Mockup · Landing Page Redesign

Low-fidelity wireframes showing the current Tata Neu homepage layout vs the proposed redesign. Each change directly addresses a problem found in user research and Play Store reviews.

CURRENT Before
1
Two separate animated banners
Rewards banner (NeuCoins) and Offers banner both animate independently at the top of the screen. Before the user can orient themselves, two competing motion elements are firing simultaneously.
2
Search bar placed too high
The search bar sits immediately below the banners, near the top of the screen. On any phone taller than ~5.5 inches, this is outside the natural one-handed thumb arc, forcing an awkward stretch or grip shift.
3
No personalisation at all
Every user sees the exact same static layout every session. A returning user who uses Grocery, Flights, and Medicines daily still has to navigate fresh each time. No recently used section, no shortcuts, no adaptive content.
PROPOSED After
1
Banners merged into one
NeuCoins rewards and promotional offers are combined into a single gradient banner. One animated element instead of two reduces visual noise and lets the user orient before anything else competes for attention.
2
Search moved into thumb zone
The search bar is now positioned below the quick-action row, placing it within the natural one-handed thumb arc (highlighted in blue). Users no longer need to stretch or shift grip to reach the most-used interaction on the screen.
3
Recents strip added below search
A row of the user's 4 most recently accessed features sits immediately below the search bar. Returning users can tap directly back to Grocery, Flights, or any other feature from the first visible screen, without navigating through categories again.

User Personas
These three personas were constructed to represent the majority of the survey sample. Tier 1 and Tier 2 users account for 87% of respondents (33 of 38) and skew 25 to 35 years old (74%). The 25-30 age bracket alone represents 50% of the sample. Sagar (Tier 2, 42) extends coverage to older, less digitally-fluent users who make up a smaller but high-value segment of Tata Neu's addressable market. Proposed New Features
Repair + Maintenance Tracking
New
Survey score: 6.76 / 10
After buying a product on Tata Neu, the natural next question when something breaks is: where do I get it repaired? Currently, users leave the app entirely and navigate to brand websites or third-party service aggregators. This feature closes that loop. Users can schedule a repair, track the technician, see service history for every product they have bought on the platform, and pay for the service - all within Tata Neu. For Tata, which already sells Croma products and has access to Tata-group service networks, the partner integrations are a natural extension of existing relationships.
Technical
Integrate partner websites on the platform; push tracking notifications for scheduled services.

DAU / MAU

Flight Price Alerts
New
Survey score: 6.37 / 10
Most users browsing flights on Tata Neu are not ready to book on the first visit. They check a price, leave, come back later, and often find the price has changed. This feature solves that loop by letting users set a route and receive an alert when the fare drops or when the ML model predicts prices are likely to rise soon. The notification carries enough information to act immediately - route, price, probability signal, and a deep-link to checkout. This directly increases flight booking conversion and gives users a reason to stay within the Tata Neu ecosystem rather than switching to MakeMyTrip or Ixigo for monitoring.
Technical
Build ML tracking pipeline for fare prediction; personalised alerts linked to user's past searches.

User Engagement

Flexible Date Flight Search
Complex
Survey score: 4.97 / 10
A significant portion of travellers have flexible dates and are optimising for price, not a fixed departure day. Currently Tata Neu requires users to pick a specific date, then manually change it and repeat the search to compare. A calendar grid showing fares across a 30-day window - similar to Google Flights - lets users instantly identify the cheapest travel window. The risk is UI complexity: a dense fare calendar can overwhelm casual users. Careful progressive disclosure (show the grid only on tap) mitigates this. Survey data shows this scores lowest among proposed features (4.97/10), which is why it ranks last in RICE prioritisation despite the genuine user need.
Technical
Heavy data processing, API integration, and UI/UX design challenges. Highest effort of all three features.

North Star Metric
Monthly Transacting Users (MTU)
The number of unique users who complete at least one transaction (purchase, payment, or booking) per month. This single metric captures both engagement and monetisation simultaneously. It rises only when the app is reliable enough to trust with money, discoverable enough to find the right feature, and valuable enough to return to. All proposed fixes and features directly ladder up to MTU growth.
